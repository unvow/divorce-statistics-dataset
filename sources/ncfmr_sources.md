# National Center for Family & Marriage Research (NCFMR)

## Overview

The NCFMR at Bowling Green State University publishes annual family profiles using American Community Survey (ACS) data, covering divorce rates by demographics and remarriage trends.

## Data Used

### First Divorce Rate, 2023 (FP-25-02)

- **Total first divorce rate:** 11.5 per 1,000 married males, 13.4 per 1,000 married females
- **Estimated first divorces:** 625,295 (males), 718,716 (females)
- **By race/ethnicity:** Black non-Hispanic highest (21.3 males, 25.7 females per 1,000 married); Asian non-Hispanic lowest (6.4 males, 7.9 females per 1,000 married)
- **Trend:** Declined from 16.9/18.7 (males/females) in 2008 to 11.5/13.4 in 2023

### Remarriage Rate, 2023 (FP-25-03)

- **Total remarriage rate:** 34.4 per 1,000 previously married males, 18.5 per 1,000 previously married females
- **Estimated remarriages:** 556,951 (males), 532,504 (females)
- **By age:** Highest for women 18-29 (100.4 per 1,000), highest for men 30-44 (73.2 per 1,000)
- **By education:** Master's degree+ had highest rates (47.0 males, 24.6 females per 1,000)
- **Trend:** Declined from 48.9/25.2 (males/females) in 2008 to 34.4/18.5 in 2023

## Notes

- All rates use ACS 1-year estimates with divorced/separated population as the base
- NCFMR family profiles are published annually and cover the most recent ACS data year
- Rates differ from CDC NVSS crude rates because the denominator is the married or previously married population, not total population
