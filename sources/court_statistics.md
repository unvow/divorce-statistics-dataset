# Court Statistics and Additional Sources

## Office of Child Support Enforcement (OCSE)

The OCSE Annual Report to Congress provides aggregate national data on child support collections and compliance rates. Note: OCSE does **not** publish average monthly payment amounts by state.

- **Data used:** National compliance context (supplementary)
- **Link:** https://www.acf.hhs.gov/css/policy-guidance/annual-report-congress

## National Center for Family & Marriage Research (NCFMR)

NCFMR at Bowling Green State University publishes research briefs on marriage and divorce trends.

- **Data used:** Historical trend context, marriage rate analysis
- **Link:** https://www.bgsu.edu/ncfmr.html

## State Court Administrative Offices

Individual state court systems publish data on case processing times, including divorce case timelines.

- **Data used:** Mandatory waiting periods (verified against state statutes), average processing times for contested and uncontested divorces, filing fee schedules
- **Data year:** 2024-2025
- **Note:** Timeline data varies by county within states; figures represent statewide averages. Statute citations are included in the CSV source column. Filing fees verified against state court fee schedules and aggregated by bestlawyersinunitedstates.com (2026 compilation).

## Martindale-Nolo Consumer Surveys

Martindale-Nolo conducts consumer surveys of divorce consumers to collect data on legal costs.

- **Data used:** Average total cost of contested and uncontested divorces (national breakdowns); state-specific estimates for 15 states
- **Data year:** 2019 (most recent published consumer survey)
- **Note:** Cost figures are self-reported by survey respondents and may not reflect all cases. National averages: uncontested $4,100; settled without trial $10,600; trial on one issue $20,400; trial on multiple issues $23,300. Average attorney fees: $11,300 (mean), $7,000 (median). Average hourly rate: $270 (2019).
- **Link:** https://www.nolo.com/legal-encyclopedia/ctp/cost-of-divorce.html

## Self.inc Divorce Cost Analysis (2025)

Self.inc published a comprehensive state-by-state analysis of average divorce costs in October 2025, covering all 50 states plus DC.

- **Data used:** Average total divorce cost per person by state; average cost with children vs. without children
- **Data year:** 2025
- **Key findings:** National average $9,969 per person; range from $6,170 (Montana) to $14,435 (California); children increase cost by 36.7% ($7,232) nationally
- **Link:** https://www.self.inc/info/cost-of-divorce-in-the-us-by-state/

## Clio Legal Trends Report / LawPay Attorney Rate Data (2023)

Clio's Legal Trends Report and LawPay publish annual data on attorney hourly billing rates by state and practice area, based on aggregated billing data from tens of thousands of legal professionals.

- **Data used:** Average attorney hourly rates by state (general practice; family law rates trend slightly lower)
- **Data year:** 2023 (published 2024)
- **Key findings:** National average hourly rate $312 for family law (Clio); $349 all practice areas (Jan 2025). Highest: DC at $392; Lowest: West Virginia at $162. State-specific rates range from $162/hr (WV) to $392/hr (DC).
- **Links:**
  - https://www.clio.com/resources/legal-trends/compare-lawyer-rates/
  - https://www.lawpay.com/about/blog/lawyer-hourly-rate-by-state/

## BestLawyersInUnitedStates.com Filing Fee Compilation (2026)

Aggregated compilation of divorce filing fees across all 50 states, cross-referenced with state court fee schedules.

- **Data used:** Filing fee ranges (min/max) for divorce petitions by state
- **Data year:** 2025-2026
- **Note:** Filing fees vary by county in some states (e.g., Alabama, Georgia, Louisiana, Tennessee). Ranges reflect known county-level variation. Some states have uniform statewide fees (e.g., California $435, Mississippi $50, Wyoming $70-85).
- **Link:** https://bestlawyersinunitedstates.com/divorce-filing-fees/
