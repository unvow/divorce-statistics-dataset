# U.S. Census Bureau Sources

## Current Population Survey — Child Support Supplement (CPS-CSS)

### Overview

The Census Bureau's CPS Child Support Supplement provides data on custodial parent demographics and custody arrangements.

### Data Used

- **Custodial parent breakdown** — 78.2% mothers, 21.8% fathers (P60-285)
- **Child support compliance** — 75.5% receiving at least some payment, 24.5% receiving nothing (P60-285)
- **Mean annual child support received** — $3,114 (2022 dollars) among custodial parents who received payments (P60-285)
- **Custodial parents with agreements or orders** — 41.0% of all custodial parents; 91% of those with agreements had formal legal orders (P60-285)

### Notes

- Report: "Custodial Parents and Their Child Support: 2022" (P60-285), published August 2025, using 2023 CPS-CSS data covering reference year 2022
- "Custodial parent" = the parent with whom the child primarily resides
- 13.9 million custodial parents total; 22.2 million children under 21 living with one parent (27.1% of all children)
- The trend toward father custody has increased from 16% in 1994 to 20.1% in 2017 to 21.8% in 2022
- Joint custody is measured separately from custodial parent designation
- Supersedes the prior report P60-269 (2017 data, published May 2020)

### Links

## Survey of Income and Program Participation (SIPP)

### Overview

The SIPP provides data on child support payment amounts received by custodial parents.

### Data Used

- **Average child support received** — $441/month ($5,292/year) among custodial parents who received payments
- **Total collected** — $20.2 billion

### Notes

- Report: "Child Support: 2021" (P70FS-185) published November 2023, using 2021 SIPP data
- A newer fact sheet covering calendar year 2023 (from 2024 SIPP data) was scheduled for release January 26, 2026
- State-level average payment data is NOT available from any single authoritative source
- OCSE does not publish average monthly payments by state

### Links

- Census Bureau SIPP: https://www.census.gov/programs-surveys/sipp.html
- SIPP Fact Sheet P70FS-185: https://www.census.gov/library/fact-sheets/2022/demo/p70fs-185.html

## American Community Survey (ACS) — Marriage and Divorce

### Overview

The ACS provides annual estimates of marriage and divorce rates per 1,000 women aged 15+, which differ from the CDC's crude rates per 1,000 total population.

### Data Used

- **Census Story (October 2024):** U.S. divorce rate fell from 9.8 to 7.1 per 1,000 women 15+ between 2012-2022
- **Marriage-to-divorce ratio:** 2.34 marriages per divorce (2022)

### Notes

- ACS rates use women 15+ as the denominator, making them higher than CDC crude rates
- These are separate and complementary to the CDC NVSS rates used in state-level data

### Links

- Census Story: https://www.census.gov/library/stories/2024/10/marriage-and-divorce.html
